What is the Future Outlook for the Internet of Things (IoT)?

The number of connected IoT devices is expected to increase substantially in the coming years. Companies are anticipated to continue embracing IoT to achieve automation and offer more intelligence. In addition, organizations adopting IoT are likely to focus on efforts to make the technology faster and smarter. Mobile devices like smartphones, tablets and other hand held devices and IoT devices that communicate with other machines are witnessing the highest growth. The proliferation of IoT technology is almost universal in virtually every sector of society, economy and life. Connected devices are today increasingly becoming ubiquitous in manufacturing plants, warehouses, business processes, logistics systems, retail stores, hospitals, public/government services, smart cities, and consumer homes, among others. Individuals and organizations, by using sophisticated computing systems and algorithms to capture, store and analyze data, could ensure selective storage and sharing of information, as well as enhance the analytical powers.


In the emerging world with more machines than people, IoT is poised to takes the center stage. As the fastest growing man made phenomenon, the rise of the machines is expanding the addressable market opportunity for IoT. The growth of machines especially mobile devices is continuing spiral at rates 5 times faster than population growth. Mobile devices like smartphones, tablets and other hand held devices and IoT devices that communicate with other machines are witnessing the highest growth. Other machines include smart meters, cars, industrial equipment etc. Today, over a large portion of IoT activity is centered in manufacturing, transportation/logistics, smart city/utility and consumer applications. Over the next two years demand response, distributed energy generation and storage, smart meters, and fleet management will emerge as largest IoT spending categories. Applications with medium-term potential include automated inventory management, and predictive equipment maintenance.


IoT technology is expected to boom in the near future. The rise of 5G is likely to impact the IoT market, as 5G technology can provide high-speed transmission capabilities to not only present but also new IoT networks. Growing security issues are likely to compel businesses to implement data security as well as privacy approach starting from the designing phase and implement it at various levels via the IoT architecture layers. Moving forward, as IoT systems generate huge amounts of data, product manufacturing companies are likely to move towards adopting a service ecosystem.

The number of connected devices being installed is anticipated to grow tremendously in the years to come, indicating a very promising, revolutionary, and innovative future for the IoT sector. Some of the trends that are expected to transform the face of IoT include: -


  • Single Interface - Today, a growing number of devices with varied capabilities are becoming part of the IoT network. Users, enterprises, or individuals would require a single interface that can enable them to access end-to-end instead of an array of interfaces and remotes.
  • Shift from Products to Services Ecosystem- Key factor driving any IoT system is the data that is created by the system. Businesses offering products are likely to move to services around the understanding given by the data.
  • Multi-Level Privacy and Security - Data security as well as privacy is very crucial. Data security, historically, has been made available at a device level such as a laptop or smartphone, or any other kind of smart device that is connected to the IoT network. Nonetheless, in the coming years, IoT is expected to be provided at various layers between IoT endpoints.
  • 5G Integration - Many companies started testing the 5G network in 2020. IoT architecture is expected to be integrated with 5G into the old as well as new systems. 5G has the potential to improve the capabilities of present network manifolds. 5G would not supersede the current network but improve its capabilities. 5G network would be able to transmit data to and from cloud servers at great speed.
  • Regulatory Landscape - Regulation is glared by technology enthusiasts and developers, however, IoT is a technology that is capable of affecting institutions and people in a way that is generally not related to any other kind of technology. This is mainly due to data, which is the major driver of any IoT system, and data is highly prone to be misused. Thus, in order to protect the data governments across the world have been issuing rules and regulations for the adoption of IoT systems.

IoT technology is becoming smarter as more and more companies incorporate AI, especially machine learning, into IoT applications to increase capabilities, including enhancing operational efficiency while avoiding unplanned downtime. The key lies in extracting insights from data. AI is making waves in IIoT with considerable investments, enterprise deployments, and new products. The integration of IoT and AI is helping organizations raise operational efficiency, prevent unplanned downtime, launch new services and products, and increase risk management. Companies devising an IoT strategy, looking to increase value from current IoT deployment, or assessing a prospective new IoT project can experiment with AI. Artificial Intelligence can extract meaningful insights from large volumes of IoT data with greater precision and more swiftly compared to conventional business intelligence solutions.


Since the two technologies complement each other, using them together can offer a host of benefits to businesses like avoiding unplanned downtime, which in turn, saves significant costs. Predictive maintenance, which uses analytics to forecast equipment failure before it happens by scheduling regular maintenance procedures, can decrease the costs related to unplanned downtime. Since AI technologies help detect anomalies and patterns, and predict events on the basis of large data sets, they are especially valuable in using predictive maintenance. The two technologies can also boost operational efficiency as machine learning offers prompt and accurate predictions along with deep insights into operations. Some AI technologies are automating an increasing number of tasks. Leading companies like Google and Hershey are combining IoT sensor data with AI to dramatically slash operational costs. IoT and AI can also be the basis on which enhanced and ultimately completely new services and products can be offered. For example, GE has turned to AI in order to automate defect identification and inspection device navigation for its drone and robotic industrial inspection services using the data collected by them. This may lead to safer, more accurate, and less-expensive inspections by saving almost 25 per cent in costs. AI and IoT can also improve risk management since many applications


combining AI with IoT are assisting companies in better understanding and predicting a range of risks while automating rapid response, which in turn, facilitates better management of worker safety, cyber threats, and financial loss. Enterprises across sectors can gain from AI technology which significantly improves the value delivered by IoT deployments, facilitating superior operations and offerings while giving their business performance an edge over competitors.


The integration of AI transforms IoT into a much smarter system, and the goal is to enable these systems to make correct judgments without the intervention of any humans. Including 5G in AIoT could possibly be one of the biggest innovations. 5G is developed for enabling the quick transfer of large data files in IoT devices via its lower latency and higher bandwidth. AIoT can aid in resolving all the current operational issues such as costs related to effective human capital management or the complexities associated with delivery models and supply chains. AI is nothing but a simulation of processes carried out by humans with the help of machines, particularly computer systems, and is mainly utilized in machine vision, speech recognition, and natural language processing. IoT on the other hand is a network of unified and consistent computing devices, objects, or digital and mechanical machines with exquisite identifiers and the capability to transmit data over the network without any human-to-computer interaction or human-to-human interaction. A thing of IoT can be a vehicle with built-in sensors for alerting the driver about low tire pressure, any object that can be equipped with an Internet protocol address and transmit data over the network, or an individual’s heart monitor implant.


AIoT is transformational and advantageous to both technologies because AI adds value to IoT via enhanced decision-making processes and machine learning capabilities and IoT adds value to AI via data exchange, signaling, and connectivity. AIoT can boost businesses as well as their services by generating more value out of IoT-created data. AI allows the IoT device to make use of collected big data for better analyzing, learning, and making decisions sans any human. In AIoT devices, AI is part of infrastructure components such as chipsets and programs that are all connected with the help of IoT networks. APIs are subsequently utilized for ensuring that software, platform components, and hardware are all operating as well as communicating with each other without the need for any end-user. When functional, IoT devices develop and collect data, while AI examines the data and offers insights, and enhances productivity and efficiencies. Insights are obtained by AI utilizing processes such as data learning. Data from IoT devices is processed in close proximity to these devices in order to decrease the bandwidth required for moving data while curtailing any likely delays in analyzing data.


Most AIoT applications focus on implementing cognitive computing in various consumer appliances. However, some of the widely uses AIoT include:


  • Smart Cities - Smart technology such as meters, light, and sensors are utilized for gathering data which is developed for helping to drive economic growth, enhance the quality of life for individuals, and increase operational efficiency.
  • Autonomous Vehicles - These vehicles depend on more than video cameras as well as sensor systems for collecting data about all the nearby automobiles, examining driving conditions, and checking for pedestrians.
  • Autonomous Delivery Robots - Sensors used in these robots collect information about the environment surrounding the robot for instance a warehouse and subsequently make use of AI for making traversal-based decisions.
  • Healthcare - Medical devices as well as wearables gather and supervise real-time health information, such as heart rate, and can eradicate irregular heartbeats.
  • Enterprise & Industrial - Manufacturers make use of smart chips for identifying if the machine is not working accurately or if any of the parts need replacement.
  • Social Media and Human Resources - AIoT tools can be incorporated into HR-related and social media platforms for creating an AI decision-as-a-service function for HR individuals.
  • Smart Home - Smart appliances gain knowledge through human interaction and their response. AIoT appliances are also capable o learning and storing information from user data for understanding consumer habits and offering personalized support.
  • Smart Retail - Retailers make use of smart cameras for recognizing shoppers’ faces and identifying if the shoppers have scanned the items purchased at the self-checkout counters prior to exiting the store.
